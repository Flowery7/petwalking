Tips & Useful Info For Healthy Pets

Medical Disclaimer: This guide is for general pet care information only and should not replace professional veterinary advice. Always consult a qualified vet for any health concerns about your pet.

As professional pet carers serving Huddersfield, Halifax, Wakefield, and across West Yorkshire, we've worked with dogs of all breeds and ages. Here's our expert guide to dog care and the most common health issues we encounter, based on UK veterinary guidance.

  • 1 Regular Exercise is Essential
    Most dogs benefit from at least 30–60 minutes of daily exercise, though requirements vary significantly by breed, age, and health. Border Collies may need 2+ hours, while brachycephalic breeds like French Bulldogs require less.
  • 2 Proper Nutrition Matters
    Feed high-quality dog food appropriate for your dog's age, size, and activity level. Avoid table scraps which can cause digestive issues and obesity.
  • 3 Regular Vet Check-ups
    Annual wellness exams are recommended for adult dogs. Senior dogs (7+ years depending on breed) should typically visit twice yearly for preventive care.
  • 4 Dental Hygiene
    Brush your dog's teeth 2-3 times weekly. Studies suggest over 80% of dogs show signs of dental disease by age 3, according to veterinary sources including The Kennel Club.
  • 5 Grooming & Coat Care
    Regular brushing removes dead hair, distributes natural oils, and helps you spot skin issues early.
  • 6 Socialisation is Key
    Well-socialised dogs are generally happier and less anxious. Introduce your dog to new experiences gradually and positively.
  • 7 Mental Stimulation
    Bored dogs may develop destructive behaviours. Use puzzle toys, training sessions, and varied walking routes to keep their mind active.
  • 8 Maintain a Healthy Weight
    Veterinary professionals estimate that nearly half of UK dogs are overweight or obese. Monitor body condition and measure food portions carefully.
  • 9 Provide a Safe Environment
    Dog-proof your home by securing toxic substances (chocolate, grapes, raisins, xylitol, onions, ibuprofen) and covering electrical cords.
  • 10 Show Love & Affection
    Positive reinforcement training builds trust and strengthens your bond with your pet.

1. Skin Allergies
Itching, hot spots, and allergic reactions are consistently among the most common veterinary presentations.
2. Ear Infections
Particularly common in floppy-eared breeds like Cocker Spaniels, Labradors, and Basset Hounds where moisture gets trapped.
3. Gastrointestinal Issues
Vomiting and diarrhoea from dietary indiscretion, infections, or other conditions are extremely common vet complaints.
4. Dental Disease
Periodontal disease, broken teeth, and oral infections are among the most frequently diagnosed conditions in dogs.
5. Arthritis & Joint Pain
Especially common in older dogs, large breeds, and overweight dogs. Signs include stiffness and difficulty climbing stairs.
6. Parasites
Fleas, ticks, and worms cause significant discomfort and can lead to secondary infections.
7. Urinary Tract Infections
Frequent urination, accidents in the house, or bloody urine are common signs needing veterinary attention.
8. Eye Problems
Conjunctivitis, corneal ulcers, and dry eye are frequent reasons for veterinary visits.
9. Obesity-Related Conditions
Diabetes, heart disease, and respiratory issues linked to excess weight are increasingly common in overweight dogs.
10. Trauma & Injuries
Cuts, broken bones, and soft tissue injuries from accidents, fights, or falls require urgent veterinary care.

Ingested toxins

Breathing difficulties

Bleeding or seizures
Uncontrolled bleeding or repeated seizures

Heatstroke signs
